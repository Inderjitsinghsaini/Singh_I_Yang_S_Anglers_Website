About Us

The TRAA Works at a Grassroots Level Within the Thames River Watershed to:

work One

protect, enhance and
regenerate a viable,
multi-species, year-round
fishery.

work Two

promote and participate in
environmental clean-up,
pollution control and
rehabilitation

work Three

breed, relocate and stock
to re-establish or augment
appropriate species of fish

work Four

promote environmentally
sound sport fishing
practices.

work Five

form partnerships with
community businesses,
government agencies,
special interest groups,
landowners, and other
stakeholders in the
Thames River watershed.

work Six

increase public awareness of
the unique diversity of the
Thames River Watershed, its
challenges, its recreational
opportunities and its vital
importance to our community.

More About TRAA

Section One

In the spring of 1986 the TRAA
was formed by anglers
concerned with the state of the
fishery in the Thames River
watershed, particularly the
dwindling smallmouth bass
population in the North Thames
River.

Section Two

The TRAA is not a fishing club,
although most members are
anglers and share fishing as a
common interest. We are a
"hands on" environmental group
who likes to work at a grass
roots level.

Continue Reading >>>

Section Three

The TRAA is a volunteer
organization, governed by a
formal constitution and by-laws.
An elected Executive, with the
guidence of the General
Membership, sets the policies
and direction for the TRAA.

Continue Reading >>>

Section Four

The TRAA has a history of always
having a core group of active
members supported by those
who just want to be part of the
solution. Either way, every
member of the TRAA is active in
their advocacy for the health of
the Thames River watershed and
its inhbitants.
